Rodent Tunneling

Holes! Holes everywhere! What should you do about all those holes in your yard and by your house?! First, stick your head in one and shut the hell up for 2 seconds while I help. Step one: cover all holes but one. Step two: fill hole with propane with a propane accessory I tell you what. Step 3: Have your LEAST favorite kid throw a match on said hole. Voila! Problem solved... and dinners done. Just kidding folks don't be sensitive I would never suggest this? In some seriousness there is not much you can do for rodents in your yard as long as they stay out there. The best advice I can give is to get rid of anything that will attract rodents such as bird feeders, chickens, pet food and your dogs poop (rodents eat the stuff!). Believe it or not there is even rodent contraceptive out there but it's so hard to get the condoms on the little buggers (is he joking?).

If the holes in the yard really bug you, fill them with sand or pea gravel. For the holes by the house this could mean rodents have tunneled under the footing and are running free in you house. There are a few tricks to stop this but I found the easiest way to solve it is to dig out the hole by the foundation down to the spot the rodents are getting under the footing and poor concrete over it. The next strategy is to dig a trench about 12" deep and fill that trench with screen shaped in the form of an L with the back of the L resting against the footing and the bottom preventing rodents from digging down anymore. This was smartly called L-screening.
